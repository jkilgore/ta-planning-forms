How to Submit an Announcement

A quick guide for ministry leaders — get your event in front of the congregation in a few steps.

The Announcement form gives the team everything they need to promote your event — slide content, a spoken script for stage, and the Sundays it should run. Submit it at least two weeks before your event date so the content team has time to prepare.

Step-by-step

1

Fill in the Event Details

Start with the basics — what is the event, who is submitting it, and when is it happening?

Event name ✱ Submitted by ✱ Event date ✱ Call to action / link

The Call to action / link is optional but recommended — add a sign-up page, website, or any URL you want people to visit.

Announcement Sundays — once you enter an event date, the form automatically calculates the two Sundays before your event when the announcement will run. You'll see them appear as date tags below the event date field — no action needed.
2

Add your bullet points

Bullet points are short, punchy facts used on slides and in the bulletin. Think of these as the essential "need to know" details — date, time, location, cost, who it's for.

Click + Add bullet point to add more rows. At least one bullet point is required. You can remove a bullet with the × button on the right.

3

Write the announcement script

This is the full spoken announcement as it will be read from stage — written in a conversational tone, as if you're talking directly to the congregation.

This field is optional, but the more detail you provide here the better the team can represent your event accurately.

4

Submit

Click Submit announcement. You'll see a green confirmation that includes the two Sundays your announcement is scheduled to run.

Helpful tips

  • Fields marked ✱ are required — you won't be able to submit without them.
  • Submit at least two weeks before your event so the content team has time to prepare slides and graphics.
  • Bullet points should be short — one line each. Save the detail for the script.
  • If the submission fails, check that you're connected to the internet and try again. Contact the operations team if it keeps failing.
